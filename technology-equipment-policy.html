Brokerage Policy

Technology Equipment Policy

Effective: April 2026 Approved by: Mike Turner, Broker of Record Applies to: All licensed agents

Royal LePage Turner Realty (2014) Inc. — serving Gander, Happy Valley–Goose Bay, and St. John’s.

1. Purpose

Royal LePage Turner Realty is committed to ensuring every agent has the tools to do their best work. Our technology stack — including SkySlope, Rechat, BoldTrail, and iGuide — requires reliable, capable hardware to function effectively. This policy establishes a consistent equipment standard across all our offices, defines brokerage support for agents who need it, and ensures fairness for agents who are already well-equipped.

2. Equipment Standard

The following represents the minimum standard required to work effectively with the brokerage’s technology tools. Agents are expected to maintain equipment that meets or exceeds this standard.

2.1 Computer

  • Recommended device: Apple MacBook Neo (512GB, Touch ID) or equivalent
  • Minimum: Any laptop or desktop capable of running a current version of macOS or Windows 11 with Chrome or Safari
  • Minimum age guideline: Devices older than 5 years should be assessed for replacement
  • Operating system: Must be within two major versions of the current release and receiving security updates

2.2 Smartphone

  • Minimum: iPhone 12 or newer (iOS current or one version behind)
  • Required for: Rechat mobile, SkySlope, BoldTrail, MLS Mobile, and client communication

2.3 Internet Connection

  • Minimum: 25 Mbps download / 10 Mbps upload
  • Recommended: 50 Mbps or higher for video calls, iGuide uploads, and large file transfers
  • Note: Starlink is an acceptable and recommended option for agents in areas with limited cable or DSL service

3. Recommended Device: MacBook Neo

Effective April 2026, the brokerage’s recommended standard laptop is the Apple MacBook Neo (512GB with Touch ID), purchased through Apple Business. This device was selected because it:

  • Handles all brokerage tools effortlessly (SkySlope, Rechat, BoldTrail, iGuide, video calls)
  • Integrates seamlessly with iPhone via AirDrop, Handoff, iPhone Mirroring, and Universal Clipboard
  • Provides up to 16 hours of battery life — suitable for full days without a charger
  • Is purchased and managed centrally through Apple Business at no additional device management cost
  • Enables zero-touch deployment: new devices arrive pre-configured and ready to use

4. Brokerage Support

4.1 Annual Technology Allowance

All active agents receive an annual technology allowance of $300 CAD, regardless of their current equipment status. This allowance may be used for:

  • Computer peripherals (monitor, keyboard, mouse, hub)
  • Phone accessories (MagSafe charger, case, mount)
  • Headphones or headset for video calls
  • Internet upgrades or equipment (router, Starlink subscription contribution)
  • Work-related software or app subscriptions
Agents submit receipts to Kayla Tulk for reimbursement. Allowance does not carry forward to the following year.

4.2 Equipment Gap Support

Agents whose equipment falls below the minimum standard (Section 2) may apply for additional brokerage support. The following applies:

  • Agents with a documented equipment deficiency may request a brokerage-subsidized device
  • Subsidized devices remain brokerage property and must be returned upon departure from the brokerage
  • Support is assessed individually by the Broker of Record
  • Priority is given to agents in remote offices (HVGB, St. John’s) who cannot access in-office equipment

4.3 Agents Already Meeting the Standard

Agents who already have equipment that meets or exceeds the standard in Section 2 are not disadvantaged. The annual technology allowance (Section 4.1) applies equally to all agents and is intended to recognize that maintaining good equipment is an ongoing investment.

5. Device Management: Apple Business

The brokerage manages all brokerage-owned devices through Apple Business, Apple’s free device management platform launching April 14, 2026. This system provides:

  • Centralized device setup using Blueprints — devices arrive pre-configured with brokerage apps and settings
  • Zero-touch deployment for remote agents: a device can be shipped directly and is ready to use out of the box
  • Remote device management, including the ability to wipe a device if it is lost, stolen, or an agent departs
  • App distribution: brokerage-approved apps can be pushed to all managed devices automatically

Agents using personally owned devices are not required to enroll in Apple Business device management, but are encouraged to do so for streamlined app setup and support.

7. Initial Technology Assessment

To establish a baseline, all agents will be asked to complete a brief technology survey distributed via Mailchimp. The survey covers:

  • Computer type and age
  • Operating system version
  • Internet connection type and speed
  • Smartphone model and age
  • Performance experience with brokerage tools

Survey results will be reviewed by the Broker of Record and Office Manager. Agents with identified gaps will be contacted individually to discuss support options. Survey responses are confidential and used solely for planning purposes.

9. Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed annually and updated as the brokerage’s technology stack evolves. Agents will be notified of any material changes. Questions regarding this policy should be directed to Mike Turner, Broker of Record.
